Sponsorship Program

Governance-Aligned Definition

Sponsorship is a formal, structured relationship between the project and an external entity (individual, organization, or corporation) that provides financial resources to support the project’s mission, infrastructure, and community.

Sponsorship is governed by the following principles:

· Transparency: All sponsorship agreements, tiers, and funds received are publicly documented.
· Non-Interference: Sponsors have no control over project direction, governance, or technical decisions.
· Reciprocal Recognition: Sponsors receive acknowledgment in proportion to their contribution level, as defined in the sponsorship tiers.
· Sustainability: Sponsorship funds are allocated to operational costs, development grants, community programs, and reserves as determined by the governance body.

Sponsorship agreements are managed through the project’s official fiscal sponsor or treasury committee and are reviewed periodically for alignment with these principles.
